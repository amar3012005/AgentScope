### Brand Tone (Textual DNA)

**The Archetype:** 
**"The Visionary yet Grounded Partner" (Der verlässliche Energie-Begleiter)**
Solvis is no longer just a "device manufacturer"; it is a provider of modular energy platforms and ecosystems. The persona is a mix of a trusted, highly precise engineer (for SHK professionals) and a warm, caring family member (for private customers). The tone is always human, inviting, and forward-thinking, translating complex energy transitions into accessible, everyday comfort.

**Vocabulary & Lexicon:**
*   **Include (Preferred Terms & Trademarks):**
    *   *Core Terms:* Use "Energiesystem" (energy system) or "Wärmesystem" (heating system) instead of "Heizung". Use "Ökosystem" (ecosystem) or "Energieplattform" (energy platform) to describe the holistic product range. Use "SHK-Fachhandwerk" or "Fachpartner" instead of "Installateure". 
    *   *Key Adjectives:* "Modular", "zukunftssicher" (future-proof), "intelligent", "effizient".
    *   *Trademarked Names:* SolvisMax, SolvisBen, SolvisLeo, SolvisPia, SolvisLea, SolvisLino, SolvisControl-3, SolvisPortal, SolvisBruno.
    *   *Brand Mantras:* "Wärme. Leben. Zukunft.", "Ein System, das mitdenkt.", "Wärme, die verbindet.", "Heute modernisieren – morgen erweitern.".
*   **Exclude (Blacklist):**
    *   Never use "Gerätehersteller" (device manufacturer).
    *   Never use "Heizkessel" or "Brenner" to describe the overall brand offering (it reduces the platform idea to a single component).
    *   Avoid isolated technical jargon without immediate translation to everyday benefits (e.g., never just list "SunSpec-Protokoll", "HEMS", or "R290" without explaining what it does for the user's comfort or independence).
    *   Never use words like "billig" (cheap), "Standard", or "kompliziert" (complicated).

**Syntactical Rules (Rhythm & Pacing):**
*   **Sentence Length Constraints:** Use short, punchy, and clear sentences. Maximum 15–20 words per sentence. The rhythm must project "Klarheit, Ruhe, Präzision" (clarity, calmness, precision).
*   **Formatting Rules:** 
    *   Always use bullet points to bundle USPs and make benefits scannable (e.g., "10 gute Gründe"). 
    *   Never use emojis in formal B2B texts; use minimal, warm emojis (like ☀️ or 🏡) only in B2C Social Media contexts if explicitly requested. 
    *   Always pair a technical feature directly with an emotional or practical benefit (Feature-Benefit pairing).

**Emotional Baseline:**
*   **Towards Private Customers (B2C):** Warm, empathetic, and reassuring. Focus on family, trust, independence, and pride. Relieve anxiety about the complex energy transition by providing clear orientation and a feeling of safety.
*   **Towards SHK Professionals (B2B):** Reliable, precise, and partnership-oriented ("auf Augenhöhe"). Focus on how Solvis makes their daily work easier, stress-free, and future-proof. 
*   **When resolving a problem or apologizing:** Be direct, transparent, and solution-oriented. Project calmness and systemic control. Do not sound panicked or overly emotional. Focus immediately on how the "intelligent system" or the "Fachpartner" will resolve the issue.

**Examples (Few-Shot Prompting):**

*   **Bad Example (Too technical, feature-focused, scattered messaging):** 
    "Der SolvisMax ist unser bester Heizkessel. Er hat eine integrierte Wärmepumpe, nutzt R290, bietet eine HEMS-Schnittstelle und senkt die Taktraten, um die Effizienz zu steigern." *(Fails because: Uses "Heizkessel", uses jargon without context, lacks emotional warmth)*
*   **Good Example (Warm, benefit-driven, platform-focused):** 
    "Ein System, das mitdenkt. Mit dem SolvisMax entscheiden Sie sich für das Herzstück Ihres neuen Energiesystems. Er verbindet Ihre Wärmepumpe intelligent mit anderen Energiequellen wie Photovoltaik. Ihr Vorteil? Sie heizen umweltfreundlich, sparen Kosten und machen sich unabhängig. Heute modernisieren – morgen erweitern." *(Succeeds because: Uses short sentences, focuses on the ecosystem, translates tech into clear benefits, uses brand mantras)*

*   **Bad Example (B2B Sales pitch):** 
    "Wir sind ein Gerätehersteller. Kaufen Sie unsere Pufferspeicher, weil sie billig und disruptiv für den Markt sind." *(Fails because: Uses banned words "Gerätehersteller", "billig", "disruptiv", lacks partnership tone)*
*   **Good Example (B2B Sales pitch):** 
    "Schluss mit Standard. Als Ihr Partner auf Augenhöhe bieten wir Ihnen keine isolierten Geräte, sondern ein modulares Energiesystem. Mit Solvis verbauen Sie präzise Technik, die Ihren Arbeitsalltag stressfrei macht und Ihren Kunden maximale Zukunftssicherheit bietet." *(Succeeds because: Speaks as an equal partner, highlights modularity, focuses on reducing stress and building trust)*
