# TextBuddy — Email Artifact Skill

## Artifact Type
Email — a single, self-contained email message ready to send.

## Template Structure

Generate the email with these sections in order:

### 1. Subject Line
- Write under 60 characters. Front-load the value proposition or key topic.
- Avoid spam triggers: no ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation, no "FREE" or "URGENT."
- Make it specific — "Q3 Pipeline Review: 3 Actions Before Friday" beats "Quick Update."

### 2. Greeting
- Match formality to the email type and brand voice.
- Cold outreach: use the recipient's first name ("Hi Sarah,").
- Internal: use first name or team name ("Hi Marketing Team,").
- Formal: use title and last name ("Dear Dr. Nguyen,").

### 3. Opening Line
- Lead with value, context, or relevance — never with "I hope this email finds you well."
- Cold outreach: reference something specific to the recipient (company news, role, shared connection).
- Follow-up: reference the prior interaction with a specific detail.
- Internal: state the purpose immediately.

### 4. Body Paragraphs
- Keep paragraphs to 2-3 sentences maximum.
- Use evidence findings to support claims — cite with `[source:ID]`.
- Build a logical flow: context, value, proof, next step.
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items — do not bury details in dense paragraphs.
- Bold key phrases sparingly for scannability.

### 5. Call to Action (CTA)
- Include exactly one clear CTA per email.
- Make the CTA specific and low-friction: "Reply with your preferred time for a 15-min call"
  beats "Let me know your thoughts."
- Place the CTA on its own line or in its own short paragraph.

### 6. Sign-Off
- Match the formality of the greeting.
- Casual: "Best," "Thanks," "Cheers,"
- Professional: "Best regards," "Kind regards,"
- Formal: "Sincerely," "Respectfully,"
- Include sender name and title. Add contact info if cold outreach.

## Tone Adaptation by Email Type

| Email Type      | Tone           | Length Target | Key Pattern                        |
|-----------------|----------------|---------------|------------------------------------|
| Cold outreach   | Warm, direct   | 80-120 words  | Personalized hook, clear value, easy CTA |
| Follow-up       | Persistent, helpful | 50-80 words | Reference prior exchange, add new value |
| Internal update | Concise, clear | 100-200 words | Purpose first, details second, action items last |
| Client update   | Professional, confident | 150-250 words | Progress summary, next steps, timeline |
| Thank you       | Genuine, brief | 40-80 words   | Specific gratitude, forward-looking close |

## Rules

- Never exceed 300 words in the email body — shorter is almost always better.
- Avoid attachments references unless evidence confirms an attachment exists.
- Do not include "Sent from my iPhone" or similar footers.
- If HITL answers specify recipient details, weave them into the personalization.
- One email per output — never generate a sequence unless explicitly asked.
