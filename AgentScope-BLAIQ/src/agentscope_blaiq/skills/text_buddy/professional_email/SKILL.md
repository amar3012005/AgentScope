# TextBuddy — Professional Email Artifact Skill

## Artifact Type
Professional email — a structured B2B or transactional email suitable for client communication, technical updates, or business correspondence.

## Template Structure

Generate the email with these sections in order:

### 1. Subject Line
- Write under 60 characters. Front-load the value proposition or key topic.
- Be specific and professional: "Modular Energy: Solvis Lea Technical Update" beats "Quick Update."
- Avoid spam triggers: no ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation, no "FREE" or "URGENT."

### 2. Salutation
- Match formality to the recipient and brand voice.
- B2B (SHK professionals): "Dear [First Name]," or "Dear [Title] [Last Name],"
- B2C (private customers): "Dear [First Name],"
- Formal/unknown: "Dear Sir or Madam," — never "To Whom It May Concern."

### 3. The 'Why' — Opening Line
- Lead with purpose in exactly 1 sentence.
- Reference something specific: a prior conversation, a shared context, or a recent development.
- Never open with "I hope this email finds you well" — it wastes the most valuable real estate.
- Cold outreach: reference the recipient's company, role, or a recent event.
- Follow-up: reference the prior interaction with a specific detail.

### 4. Key Specs / Evidence
- Present 2-3 bullet points of high-impact evidence or specifications.
- Each bullet: specific, concrete, no fluff.
- Cite evidence findings with `[source:ID]`.
- Pair technical features with practical benefits (Feature-Benefit pairing):
  - "Modular design — scale from 5 kW to 50 kW without replacing the core unit."
  - "SolvisControl-3 integration — monitor and optimize your entire energy platform from one interface."

### 5. Value Proposition
- Explain why this matters for the recipient's specific needs in 1-2 paragraphs.
- Use "you/your" more than "we/our" — client-focused framing.
- Connect the specs to outcomes: cost savings, efficiency, future-proofing, peace of mind.
- Cite evidence for claims: case studies, benchmarks, or proven results `[source:ID]`.

### 6. Call to Action (CTA)
- Include exactly one clear, soft-but-direct CTA.
- Make it specific and low-friction: "Reply with your preferred time for a 15-min call"
  beats "Let me know your thoughts."
- Place the CTA on its own line or in its own short paragraph.
- Examples: "Schedule a site visit," "Request technical documentation," "Reply to discuss."

### 7. Sign-Off
- Match the formality of the salutation.
- B2B: "Best regards," or "Kind regards,"
- B2C: "Warm regards," or "Best,"
- Include sender name and title. Add contact info if cold outreach.

## Tone Rules (Brand-Aligned)

- **B2B (SHK professionals):** Reliable, precise, partnership-oriented ("auf Augenhöhe"). Focus on making their work easier, stress-free, and future-proof.
- **B2C (private customers):** Warm, empathetic, reassuring. Focus on family, trust, independence, and pride.
- **Sentence length:** Short, punchy, clear. Maximum 15-20 words per sentence. Project "Klarheit, Ruhe, Präzision."
- **No emojis** in formal B2B emails. Minimal, warm emojis (☀️, 🏡) only in B2C if explicitly requested.
- **Never use banned words:** "Gerätehersteller," "Heizkessel," "billig," "Standard," "kompliziert."
- **Always pair technical features with emotional or practical benefits.**

## Formatting Rules

- Output as clean markdown — no HTML, no code blocks.
- Keep paragraphs to 2-3 sentences maximum.
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items.
- Bold key phrases sparingly for scannability (1-3 per email).
- Target 150-250 words for the email body.

## Rules

- Never exceed 300 words in the email body.
- Never fabricate metrics, specs, or results — all numbers must cite evidence.
- If HITL answers specify recipient details, weave them into the personalization.
- One email per output — never generate a sequence unless explicitly asked.
- Do not include "Sent from my iPhone" or similar footers.
