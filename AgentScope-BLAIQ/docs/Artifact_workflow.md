PITCH DECK: Detailed Tactical Execution
Use Case
Raising capital, selling to enterprise, stakeholder alignment meeting.
Phase 1: Research (1-2 days)
Deep Research Agent outputs:

Market size + growth rate (YoY) — aim for analyst report (Gartner, IDC, Forrester)
Customer pain point quantification (% of teams experiencing X, cost of X)
Trend data (adoption curve, market inflection points)
Use case metrics (ROI calculation, time-to-value benchmarks)

Competitive Agent outputs:

Positioning statements of 5-8 direct competitors
Their messaging (headline + 3-line value prop)
Feature vs outcome framing (how do they talk about similar features?)
Pricing strategy (if public)
Proof points they use (customers, metrics, certifications)

Insight Synthesis outputs:

10-12 Tier 1 insights (surprising, differentiated, data-backed)
5-7 Tier 2 insights (credibility/reassurance)
3-4 psychological insights (what removes friction from decision?)

Example: Building a pitch for a SaaS data infrastructure company:
TIER 1 INSIGHTS
- "Market growing 47% YoY; competitors still on legacy approach" 
  Source: Gartner Q1 2026, competitor audit
  Why it matters: Shows market momentum + our forward-thinking positioning

- "91% of enterprises cite integration speed as the deciding factor"
  Source: Forrester VoC study
  Why it matters: Addresses key buyer concern; validates our product priority

- "Average setup time: 6 weeks → our customers: 2 days"
  Source: customer data, public benchmarks
  Why it matters: Quantified proof of our core differentiator

TIER 2 INSIGHTS (Credibility)
- "Built by team with 30+ years combined infrastructure experience"
  Source: team bios
  Why it matters: Reassures technical buyers

- "SOC 2 Type II certified, GDPR compliant, EU-native infrastructure"
  Source: compliance docs
  Why it matters: Removes regulatory friction for enterprise deals

PSYCHOLOGICAL INSIGHTS (Friction removal)
- "Most teams are afraid of expensive, long-running pilots that go nowhere"
  Source: sales conversations
  Action: Reframe around guaranteed results within 30 days or full refund
HITL Checkpoint 1 (1 day)
Approval checklist for deck research:
Accuracy:
☐ All growth rates checked against primary source (analyst report, SEC filing)
☐ Customer data anonymized or has permission
☐ Competitive features/pricing current (checked their websites today?)
☐ No made-up statistics ("most companies," "industry standard") without source

Relevance:
☐ Does this research speak to the actual investor we're pitching?
☐ Market size + growth rate answer: "Why now? Why you?" narrative
☐ Competitive positioning is clear (not generic "we're faster, cheaper, better")
☐ Message hierarchy makes sense (most important insight first)

Brand Alignment:
☐ Tone appropriate for our stage (seed = scrappy/vision; Series B = execution; IPO = stability)
☐ Positioning consistent with our prior messaging
☐ No claims we'd be embarrassed to defend in a customer meeting

APPROVE & PROCEED → Phase 2
Phase 2: Optimization (2-3 days)
AIDA Mapping Agent (slide-by-slide outline):
SLIDE 1: TITLE SLIDE
A = ATTENTION
  Hook: "Ship 3x faster. No infrastructure overhead."
  Why it works: Specific benefit (3x vs "faster"), implies solution without saying it
  Duration: This is slide 1. Investors see headline instantly.

SLIDE 2: THE PROBLEM
I = INTEREST (establish why this matters NOW)
  Macro trend: "Infrastructure is the new bottleneck in software development"
  Evidence: "Market growing 47% YoY; most teams still manual" + stat/chart
  Stakes: "Talented engineers spending 20% of time on ops instead of features"
  Why now: "AI acceleration is forcing every company to move faster"

SLIDE 3-4: SOLUTION + PROOF
D = DESIRE (paint picture of better future)
  Vision: "What if setup took hours instead of weeks?"
  Proof: Case study — [customer] deployed in 2 days, saved 6 weeks
  Metrics: "40 hours/week freed for product work per team"
  
  Slide 4 = "how we're different"
  vs Competitor A: "They require on-prem setup; we're fully cloud"
  vs Competitor B: "They're expensive at scale; we have flat-rate pricing"

SLIDE 5: MARKET + TRACTION
D = DESIRE (quantify the opportunity)
  Market size: "$12B infrastructure market, growing 47% YoY"
  TAM breakdown: "Enterprise 60%, Mid-market 30%, Startups 10%"
  Traction: "[N] customers in [months], [$ ARR], [growth rate]"
  
SLIDE 6: TEAM
D = DESIRE (reinforce credibility)
  Founder 1: "15 years infra eng at Google, built X that serves Y users"
  Founder 2: "Led product at [scale-up], grew from 0→100 customers"
  Vision statement: "Infrastructure should never be the bottleneck to shipping."

SLIDE 7: BUSINESS MODEL + UNIT ECONOMICS
D = DESIRE (show path to profitability)
  Pricing: "$X/month, no setup fees, no surprises"
  Gross margin: "[Y]% (LTV:CAC = 3:1)"
  Growth: "Targeting [Z] ARR by end of 2027"

SLIDE 8: THE ASK + USE OF FUNDS
A = ACTION (remove friction from next step)
  Amount: "Raising $X million"
  Use breakdown: "[A]% product, [B]% sales/marketing, [C]% ops"
  What's next: "This round funds us to [specific milestone]"
  Urgency: "Closing this round end of Q2 2026"

SLIDE 9: CONTACT
A = ACTION (make saying yes easy)
  "Let's talk" — email, website, calendar link
  "No technical deep-dives needed to start — ask us anything"
Messaging Framework Agent (exact copy):
SLIDE 1 HEADLINE (the entire pitch in one sentence)
"Ship 3x faster. No infrastructure overhead."

SLIDE 1 SUBHEADLINE (optional, clarifies)
"Leading teams are deploying production systems in hours, not weeks."

SLIDE 2 HEADLINE
"Why infrastructure is holding you back"

SLIDE 2 BODY BULLETS
- "Average setup: 6 weeks per new deployment"
- "Ongoing maintenance: 20% of engineering time"
- "One breaking change cascades across your stack"

SLIDE 3 HEADLINE
"Here's how we changed that"

SLIDE 3 CALL-OUT (big visual, small text)
"2 days
instead of
6 weeks"

SLIDE 4 HEADLINE
"Why we're different"

vs COMPETITOR A: "On-prem only" → OUR ANGLE: "Fully cloud-native, scales to any workload"
vs COMPETITOR B: "Usage-based pricing" → OUR ANGLE: "Flat-rate, no surprise bills"
vs MARKET: "Manual management" → OUR ANGLE: "Fully automated infrastructure"

[Rest of copy follows this pattern]
Visual Direction Agent:
AESTHETIC MOOD: "Ambitious but grounded"
- Not hype-y (no neon, no Web3 vibes)
- Not corporate (no stock photography, no "people in meetings")
- Confident execution (Apple product design energy)

COLOR PALETTE
Primary: Deep blue (#0052CC) — trust, technology
Secondary: Coral (#FF6B35) — action, momentum
Neutral: Charcoal + off-white

TYPOGRAPHY
Headlines: Bold geometric sans (Clash Grotesk, Space Mono — not Inter)
Body: Clean sans (Inter, Roboto)
Hierarchy: 48px hero / 32px section headers / 16px body

VISUAL MOTIFS
- Geometric shapes: circles, triangles, grid patterns (represent structure + growth)
- Data visualization: custom charts (never Powerpoint templates)
- Animation: fade-in on scroll, subtle movement (300ms, no bounce)
- Imagery: Real team photos, real customers (no staging)

DESIGN SYSTEM TOKENS
Spacing: 8px base (matches web design)
Radius: 8px subtle corners (not sharp, not pill-shaped)
Shadows: None or very subtle (0 4px 8px, 5% opacity max)
HITL Checkpoint 2 (1 day)
Message Clarity:
☐ Can I explain this deck in one sentence? (should match Slide 1 headline)
☐ Does every slide headline make intuitive sense without the subtext?
☐ AIDA flow works? (each slide moves buyer closer to saying yes?)

Brand Alignment:
☐ This is how we talk (tone matches company voice)
☐ This is what we stand for (positioning is clear)
☐ We're ready to defend these claims in a meeting

Persuasion:
☐ Problem slide makes investor nod (they see the pain)
☐ Solution slide is memorable (clear visual + specific claim)
☐ Proof is credible (not cherry-picked; shows real traction)
☐ Ask is clear (amount + use + timeline specific)

Feasibility:
☐ Timeline OK? (slide 3 design, slide 5 chart, etc. realistic?)
☐ Assets available? (customer photos, logos, data for charts?)
☐ Animation scope reasonable? (not 40 fancy transitions)?

APPROVE → Phase 3
Phase 3: Design (3-5 days)

BODY

VISUAL APPROACH
- Simple boxes with icons (not flags, not generic "VS" graphics)
- Color: competitor boxes in neutral gray, our box in primary orange
- Icons: setup (⚙️ analogy), pricing (💰 analogy), support (💬 analogy) — clean outline style

NOTES
- Focus on our unique strengths (don't trash competitors)
- Make comparison visual (users should scan in 3 seconds)
- Our box should be slightly larger/more prominent (not arrogant, just clear)

LANDING PAGE: Detailed Tactical Execution
Use Case
SaaS product launch, free trial signup, webinar registration.
Key Differences from Pitch Deck
AspectPitch DeckLanding PageGoalPersuade an investor/buyer in 20 minutesConvert a visitor to lead in 30 secondsStructureLinear narrative (A→I→D→A)Funnel (hero → interest → proof → CTA)DepthDetails matter; read bullet pointsSkim-able; large headlines + white spaceCTASingle ask ("Let's talk")Multi-stage CTAs (hero + mid-page + footer)TimelineUsers arrive with contextUsers arrive cold; need context fast
Phase 1 & 2: Research + Optimization
Focus areas (different from pitch deck):
Research phase should prioritize:

Audience psychology: What keeps them up at night? What would make them try a tool?
Conversion patterns: How do successful SaaS landing pages in our category structure flow?
Objection mapping: What stops someone from clicking "Sign up"? (too good to be true? unclear? risky?)
Competitive differentiation: What makes someone choose us vs the 5 competitors on their comparison sheet?

Optimization phase should focus on:

Hero message: Can we communicate the value prop in 5 words? ("Connect your Slack to CRM" not "Unified data symphony")
Social proof strategy: What will make a cold visitor trust us? (customer logos, testimonials, case studies, or viral traction?)
Objection removal: How do we answer "What's the catch?" (pricing transparency, no-credit-card signup, 14-day free trial)
CTA progression: Hero CTA (high intent: "Try Free"), mid-page CTA (medium interest: "See how"), footer CTA (last chance: "Get started")

Phase 3: Design
Section-by-section spec (Landing page structure):
HERO SECTION (Above fold)
Goal: Stop the scroll
H1: "Ship your AI agent in minutes, not months"
Subheading: "No infrastructure. No DevOps. Just code."
CTA Button: "Try Free — No credit card" (orange, prominent)
Visual: Hero image or video (product screenshot, animated product demo, or lifestyle image of people shipping)
Background: Subtle gradient or clean white (not cluttered)

PROBLEM SECTION
Goal: Validate the pain
Header: "Why teams choose X over building from scratch"
3-column layout:
  Col 1: Icon + "Save months of infra work" + brief explanation
  Col 2: Icon + "Deploy to your own cloud" + explanation
  Col 3: Icon + "Support from day 1" + explanation
Visual: Icons (outline style, consistent sizing, orange + blue accent)

  Step 1: "Connect your LLM" + screenshot

  Step 3: "Deploy with one click" + deploy animation
  Step 4: "Monitor live performance" + dashboard screenshot

HOW CUSTOMERS USE IT (Case Study teaser)
"Watch how [Customer Name] deployed their AI compliance agent in 3 days"
Customer logo + quote + metric highlight ("Saves 40 hours/week")
CTA: "Read the full case study →"

  • Agent memory (persistent conversation context)

POSTER / SOCIAL ASSET: Quick Turnaround
Timeline: 3-5 days (Single-pass mode)
Phase 1: Research (1 day)
Skip deep research. Focus on:

Trend data: What's resonating in the category? (scroll LinkedIn, Twitter, competitor social)
Audience sentiment: What's the feeling people want? (urgency, aspiration, FOMO, humor, authority?)
Meme/reference analysis: What visual metaphors are already working in this space?

Example: Marketing agency creating a poster for "agency growth" service:
Trend insight: "Agencies are tired of 'do more with less' messaging"
Audience sentiment: Want to feel ambitious + in control, not overwhelmed
Reference: Successful posters in this space use bold typography + asymmetry + orange/blue combos
Unique angle: Instead of "grow faster," message is "stop burning out your team"
Phase 2: Optimization (1 day)
Single focused headline (not body copy):
OPTION A (Benefit-focused): "Ship more, manage less"
OPTION B (Fear removal): "Stop losing your best people"
OPTION C (Aspiration): "The agency that doesn't burn out"

→ Test with core team; pick strongest
Visual direction (mood + colors):
Mood: Confident but relatable (not corporate, not scrappy)
Colors: Orange (action) + charcoal (stability) on white
Typography: Bold sans serif headline (Clash, Space Mono) + clean body (Inter)
Visual motif: Geometric shapes or bold illustration (not photography)
Phase 3: Design (2-3 days)
Create multiple variants for A/B testing:
Variant 1 (Bold geometric):
  Headline: Large (120px) + orange
  Subheading: "Stop the burnout cycle"
  Visual: Abstract circles/triangles, asymmetric layout
  CTA: "Learn how →" (small, bottom-right)

Variant 2 (Minimalist):
  Headline: Smaller (80px) + black
  Subheading: Minimal (6 words max)
  Visual: Clean white space, one accent color
  CTA: Subtle link

Variant 3 (Illustrative):
  Headline: Orange
  Visual: Simple illustration (team celebrating, growing plant, etc.)
  Subheading: Benefit statement
Export & Deploy

For LinkedIn: 1200x627px, PNG, lightweight (<2MB)
For Twitter: 1024x512px, PNG
For Print: 2400x1200px at 300DPI, PDF
For Email: 600px wide, responsive

A/B test on LinkedIn for 1 week, pick winner, iterate.

Final Checklist: Before You Start
☐ Do we have a locked-in brand DNA? (colors, fonts, voice spec)
☐ Is the decision-maker (founder/CEO/CMO) available for HITL checkpoints?
☐ Are we prepared to spend 2-3 weeks? (or do we need single-pass mode?)
☐ Do we have customer/audience data to research? (not just internet research)
☐ Is the artifact type clear? (pitch vs landing vs case study?)
☐ Who owns what? (researcher, content director, designer named?)
☐ Success metric defined? (demo bookings for pitch, signups for landing page, etc.?)

IF ALL YES → Ready to start research phase
IF ANY NO → Clarify before beginning
